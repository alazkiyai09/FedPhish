Research Vision

I research privacy-preserving machine learning systems that enable financial institutions to collaborate without exposing sensitive customer data.

My work focuses on three key challenges: (1) verifiable training using zero-knowledge proofs to ensure model integrity, (2) Byzantine resilience against malicious participants in federated systems, and (3) regulatory compliance through differential privacy and homomorphic encryption.

With 3+ years of experience in banking fraud detection and published research in cryptographic methods, I bring both practical domain knowledge and theoretical depth to advance the state-of-the-art in secure federated learning.
